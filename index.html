The Pilgrimage Collection

Named for places
that inspire pilgrimage.

Inspired by sacred places around the world. Each loaf draws from a place, a tradition, and the flavors that gather people around the table.

The Pilgrimage Collection is named for sacred sites that people travel thousands of miles to visit: Fátima, Lourdes, Guadalupe, Santiago, Kibeho. Each place carries real meaning, and every loaf is named for one of them, baked with the same care and patience those places inspire.

Good to Know

Frequently asked questions

Where can I see your menu?

Our full Pilgrimage Collection of five sourdough boules plus seasonal extras is right on the order page. We bake in Liberty Hill and serve nearby customers in Santa Rita Ranch, Leander, Georgetown, Cedar Park, and North Austin through Friday preorders, market pickup, and limited local delivery.

Are your ingredients organic?

We prioritize organic, foundational ingredients. Our bread products are principally organic, while the specialty loaves use high quality, small batch ingredients that aren't all certified organic. Every boule is naturally leavened, with no preservatives and no commercial yeast.

How big are the loaves, and are they sliced?

Our standard Fatima boule is roughly 800g, a generous round loaf made to share. We bake them whole and unsliced, so you get the full crackling crust and a longer shelf life. Slice it fresh at home whenever you're ready.

Do you offer pickup or delivery?

Both. Curbside pickup is Fridays from 9am to 12pm at our Liberty Hill pickup location. Delivery is Thursdays from 3pm to 5pm from the Santa Rita Ranch area within a 10 mile radius to supported residences in Liberty Hill, Leander, and Georgetown for a flat $10. We'll ask you to text us at (512) 299-1241 when you're on your way, so each loaf is as fresh as possible.

How should I store my bread?

Keep your loaf at room temperature in a paper bag or wrapped in a clean kitchen towel, and enjoy it within two to three days. Avoid the fridge, as it dries bread out. To keep it longer, slice and freeze in an airtight bag for up to two months, then toast or warm before serving.

When do you bake, and when do orders close?

We bake fresh every Thursday, with Thursday delivery from 3pm to 5pm and Friday curbside pickup from 9am to 12pm. Orders close Wednesday evening so we have time to mix, ferment, and bake each loaf with care. Our dough takes about three days from start to finish.

Do you have allergen information?

All loaves contain gluten (wheat flour). Several contain dairy: Lourdes (Gruyère), Guadalupe (cheddar), Santiago (Manchego), and Kibeho (butter). Guadalupe also contains pepitas (pumpkin seeds), so anyone with a nut or seed allergy should avoid it. If you have specific concerns, message us before ordering and we'll help.

Do you take catering or bulk orders?

Not at this time. We're a small home bakery focused on our weekly Thursday bake. For questions about a larger order, reach out at hello@fatimabakery.com and we'll do our best to help.

Is this a licensed bakery?

Yes. Fatima Bakery ATX is a licensed Texas Cottage Food Operation, baked with care in our home kitchen in Liberty Hill.

Still have questions?

Reach us anytime at hello@fatimabakery.com or send a DM on Instagram @fatimabakeryatx.

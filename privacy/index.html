Privacy Policy

Last updated July 2026

Fatima Bakery ATX is a small home bakery in Liberty Hill, Texas. We respect your privacy and keep things simple. This policy explains what we collect when you place an order or contact us, why we collect it, and how we handle it.

What we collect

When you place an order, join Loaf Reserve Membership, or send us a message, we may collect your name, email address, phone number, Instagram handle (if you choose to share it), your pickup or delivery preference and date, and any notes you add to your order.

Why we collect it

We use this information only to prepare and fulfill your order, arrange pickup or delivery, send you an order confirmation and a link to pay, and answer any questions you send us. If you join our email list, we use your email to let you know when orders open or when we will be at a market near you.

How your information is stored

Order and contact details are stored in Google Sheets and Gmail, which we use to run the bakery. We do not sell, rent, or trade your information to anyone, ever.

Payments

We do not collect or store any card or bank information on this website. Payment happens separately through Cash App, Venmo, or Square using a link we send you by email. Those services handle your payment details under their own privacy policies.

Email and text messages

We may email you about your order or, if you opt in, about upcoming bakes and markets. On pickup or delivery day we may text the number you provide to coordinate handoff. You can ask us to stop at any time.

Keeping or removing your information

We keep order records only as long as we need them to run the bakery. If you would like us to remove your information, email hello@fatimabakery.com and we will take care of it.

Children

Our website and ordering are intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Questions

If you have any questions about your information, reach us anytime at hello@fatimabakery.com or on Instagram @fatimabakeryatx.

This policy is provided for general information and is not legal advice. As a small cottage bakery we keep our practices simple and honest. If your needs are more complex, please consult an attorney.
